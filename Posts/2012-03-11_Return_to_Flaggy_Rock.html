Return to Flaggy Rock...

It seemed like an excellent idea at the time; go to our block at Flaggy Rock in central Queensland and plant 3000 Spotted Gum trees. It shouldn't take more than a few weeks, six weeks max. Admittedly it has been a while since we had been to visit the block. With two of the biggest wet seasons in decades back to back, nature had decided to reclaim our block...

We arrived at front gate of the block on Sunday 11th March 2012, full of dreams and enthusiasm...

The front gate looking from outside in. Not been used in a while...

We brave the jungle in our wellies to get a closer look at the sheds; sort of......

Ah yes definatley overgrown...

We walk a little further into the block the vegetation is becoming more extreme. we think Mum's orchard is in there . Well that's about where it used to be...

A track into the wilderness...

How are ever going to tame this jungle. There is 160 acres of it...

On the way back to car we encounter two large angry dogs. Just got in before they got to us. Weeks later we discover that they belong to the mechanic that rents a house from out nextdoor neighbour about 1km back up the road. Time to check into the Carmila Caravan Park which will be our home for the duration. How ever long that will be...

Before we left Brisbane we when to Bunnings and bought a brushcutter, a chainsaw, a machette, a metal rake and two pairs of wellies. This is our entire equipment list to clear the jungle. Our first job is to find the survey markers on the front gate. We have the survey plan; how hard can it be. This is March in north Qld and the days are long, hot and humid. We det going at sunrise. ...

John fires up the brushcutter and attempts to cut a path through the grass on the verge to get to the front gate...

There it is our street number; 225 Douglas Rd, Carmila, Qld 4039. The district has been Flaggy Rock for over 100 years but Australia Post has decided that it is Carmila now...

After an couple of hours of grass slashing we cut track our into one of sheds...

Who's game to go in there?...

This what is in this room including two python. One directly above the doorway and one in the far corner. For obvious reasons it was named the snake room...

Looking south out of the shed...

The vines have gone feral...

The roof leaks big time. Maybe we could turn them into an artistic skylight?...

In there is our electricity meter box. It is full encased in Lanata. THAT'S why Ergon has not been able to read the meter for a while...

Ergon meterbox; take two...

Day 3 we start to try to find our boundary. We have the survey plan, a compass and 50m tape. But there are no survey pegs left anywhere to give a starting point. Well we don't think there are, we spend hours hacking around in the long grass just trying to find some point of reference...

While John tries with brute force to find a peg, Jenelle takes her map reading prowess to legendary levels when she finds the bore house concealed in long grass. Because the bore is marked on the survey plan, we now have a reference point to start our survey......

The brushcutter man leads the way under the careful guidance of his trusty map reading wizard wife...

The sheds are 3m high. Only the roof is visible...

After about 5 hours of exploring we decide on a leisurely walk along the southern boundary, or roughly what we think is the boundary, down to the beach. Jenelle has not been here for about 15 years. We think the fuel tank is at one of the dozen or so corners of our block......

Next day we decide we should head to the beach for a look...
